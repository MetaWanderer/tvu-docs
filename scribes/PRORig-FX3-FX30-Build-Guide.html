Assembly
Step-by-Step Assembly
1
Mount the Cage
Attach the SmallRig Cage to the camera body and install the cable clamps.
a
Attach the SmallRig FX3/FX30 Cage to the camera body, securing with 3 screws — 1 on the left, 1 on the right, 1 on the underside.
b
Install the top rail onto the camera body using 2 screws. Make sure the hot shoe port is not covered.
c
Install the SmallRig HDMI Cable Clamp onto the side of the camera body.
d
Install the SmallRig USB-C & Multi Cable Clamp onto the side of the camera body.
📸
Screenshot — Cage attached with screws and cable clamps installed
2
Attach Hand Strap & Handles
Add the hand strap and handles for ergonomic grip and maneuverability.
a
Attach the SmallRig Hand Strap to improve stability and prevent dropping during streaming.
b
Attach the SmallRig Side Handle using the locating pins for a secure grip.
c
Attach the SmallRig Top Handle using the locating pins for enhanced maneuverability.
💡
Both handles can be added or removed as needed depending on the stream and accessories.
📸
Screenshot — Side handle and top handle attached with locating pins
3
Install Lens & UV Filter
Mount the lens to the camera body and attach the UV filter for lens protection.
a
Work quickly in a dust-free location. Remove the body cap and the rear lens cap from the lens.
b
Align the two white index marks on the lens and camera. Hold with the lens mount facing down to prevent dust from entering.
c
Push the lens lightly toward the camera and turn slowly in the arrow direction until it clicks into the locked position.
d
Remove the front lens cap and screw on the K&F Concept Nano X-Series UV Filter. Re-install the lens cap over the filter.
⚠️
Always mount and remove the lens quickly in a clean environment to prevent dust from entering the sensor.
📸
Screenshot — Lens aligned on white index mark, UV filter attached
4
Set Up the DJI Mic 2 Audio System
Slide the receiver onto the camera hot shoe — transmitters auto-pair.
a
Slide the DJI Mic 2 Camera Adapter into the hot shoe port on top of the camera.
b
Connect the DJI Mic 2 receiver by sliding it down onto the Camera Adapter where the flat golden pins line up.
✅
The transmitters are auto-paired with the receiver. The Sony camera will automatically switch to wireless mic audio when the adapter is connected.
📸
Screenshot — DJI Mic 2 receiver seated on hot shoe adapter
5
Power the Rig
Install both the internal battery and the external V-Mount battery system.
a
Insert the Sony NP-FZ100 battery into the compartment on the underside of the camera.
b
Attach the SmallRig V-Mount Battery Plate to the underside of the camera cage.
c
Slide the SmallRig VB50 battery into the Battery Plate.
d
Connect the VB50 to the camera's USB-C port via the USB-C Right-Angled Charging Cable (22cm).
💡
The VB50 supports hot-swapping. Larger capacity models (VB99, VB212) are also compatible.
📸
Screenshot — V-Mount battery plate attached, USB-C cable connected
6
Mount on Tripod (Optional)
Use the Arca-Swiss quick release system to mount to the SmallRig VT-20.
a
Attach the tripod quick release plate to the underside of the Battery Plate using the 1/4" screw. Tighten firmly.
b
Slide the rig onto the SmallRig VT-20 Aluminum Mini Tripod using the Arca-Swiss rail to lock it in place.
📸
Screenshot — Rig mounted on VT-20 tripod via quick release plate
Configuration
Camera Settings

Press the physical MODE button → rotate the wheel or use the touchscreen → set to Movie mode before adjusting these settings.

Setup › Area/Date › NTSC/PAL Selector
Set Region to NTSC
Cameras purchased in Europe default to PAL (25/50fps). Switch to NTSC to enable 30fps and 60fps frame rates.
Set to: NTSC
Setup › Power Setting Option › Power Save Start Time
Disable Power Save
Prevent the camera from sleeping during long streaming sessions.
Power Save Start Time → Off
Setup › Power Setting Option › Auto Power OFF Temp
Set Overheating Threshold to High
Prevents the camera from shutting down during extended streams.
Auto Power OFF Temp → High
Setup › External Output › HDMI Info. Display
Disable HDMI Info Display
Turns off on-screen overlays so your HDMI output is a clean video signal with no menus visible.
HDMI Info. Display → Off
Setup › External Output › CTRL FOR HDMI
Disable HDMI Control
CTRL FOR HDMI → Off
Shooting › Image Stabilization › SteadyShot
Image Stabilization Mode
Standard — Moderate stabilization, minimal image crop. Best for general handheld use.
Active — Stronger stabilization with slight crop. Best for walking shots or dynamic movement.
SteadyShot Adjust → Auto
Shooting › Zoom › Zoom Range
Enable Clear Image Zoom
Extends zoom beyond the optical range while maintaining image quality. Useful for prime lenses. Use the Zoom Lever (W/T) to zoom.
Zoom Range → Clear Image Zoom
Shooting › Shooting Mode › Exposure Ctrl Type
Set Exposure Control Mode
Change to P/A/S/M Mode first. Then set Exposure Mode to Shutter Priority for most streams — locks in consistent motion while camera adjusts aperture and ISO.
Exposure Ctrl Type → P/A/S/M
Main › Main1 › Rec Frame Rate
Frame Rate & Shutter Speed
Set frame rate to 59.94p or 29.97p (or 23.98p for a filmic look).
Set shutter speed to double your frame rate:
 • 59.94p → 1/125
 • 29.97p → 1/60
Set ISO to ISO Auto. Set Picture Profile to PPOFF.
Audio
DJI Mic 2 Settings

Turn on the receiver → swipe down on the screen to access settings.

Receiver Settings › Recommended Camera Settings
Set Camera Brand & Model
Select Brand: Sony then select your model (FX3 or FX30). Swipe up to exit.
Receiver Settings › Power On/Off Camera Simultaneously
Enable Camera Power Sync
When enabled, the receiver and transmitters power on/off with the camera — saves battery and simplifies your workflow.
Set to: On

Help
Troubleshooting & Tips
📺 How do I fold out the camera display?
Loosen the silver thumb screw on the Battery Plate to lower the V-Mount Battery to horizontal. Fold out and rotate the touchscreen. Raise the battery back up and re-tighten. Reverse this when ending your stream to protect the display.
🔩 Handle or accessory feels loose
Ensure all screws are fully tightened. A hex key may be required for some connections.
🔋 Battery life management
Keep spare V-Mount batteries charged between shoots. The VB99 and VB212 offer higher capacity and are fully compatible with the Battery Plate.
🔌 HDMI cable wear
HDMI cables need periodic replacement. Always carry spares. Use the SmallRig HDMI Clamp to reduce stress on the camera port and extend cable life.
🔭 Lens maintenance
Regularly use the K&F Concept Lens Cleaning Pen to maintain optical clarity. Clean in a circular motion from center outward.
